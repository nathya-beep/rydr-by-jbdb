# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences who share one identity: the rider.

- **Urban riders 18–30** — Surron/e-bike scene, CDMX/NY/Brussels street culture, heavy social media, buys on impulse and tribe signal. Wants gear that reads as community membership, not just clothing.
- **E-bike enthusiasts 30–45** — more disposable income, discerning. Buys selectively. Values craft, origin story, and limited availability. Treats a RYDR piece as a statement, not a purchase.

Both groups share one context: they ride. The product is worn on bikes, shown on bikes, photographed on bikes. It lives in motion.

## Product Purpose

RYDR is a streetwear brand born from e-bike culture. It exists to give riders a brand that reflects who they actually are — not a sports brand that tolerates riders, not a hype brand co-opting the aesthetic, but something that came from the pavimento.

Success looks like: a rider in CDMX pulls up to a group ride and gets asked where they got the hoodie. Not "what brand is that" but "where do I buy one."

## Brand Personality

**Raw. Exclusive. Electric.**

Tone: confident, direct, no fluff. The brand does not explain itself. It does not use superlatives. It states what it is and trusts the rider to get it.

Emotional goal: pride of membership. The feeling of belonging to something that not everyone can access — not because it's expensive, but because it's specific.

## Anti-references

- **Nike / Adidas / mainstream sportswear**: too clean, too mass market, too optimized for everyone. RYDR is not for everyone.
- **Specialized / Trek / functional cycling brands**: technical, utilitarian, zero style identity. Riders wear this stuff because they have to, not because they want to.
- Avoid: gradient-heavy hero sections, product-catalog genericness, "Powered by community" marketing language, sport performance aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned, not broadcast** — Drop culture logic. Nothing shouts availability. Scarcity is the default posture.
2. **Culture before category** — The rider identity comes first; the clothing is the artifact. Every visual choice reinforces the world, not the SKU.
3. **Street luxury, not street fashion** — The gap between fast-fashion streetwear and this brand lives in craft, restraint, and specificity. Premium execution, no apologies.
4. **Static energy** — The brand lives in motion (riders, streets, volts) but the design itself is controlled and deliberate. Tension between stillness and speed is the visual language.
5. **One palette, total commitment** — Black and neon yellow are not design choices; they are the brand. Every element either uses them or gets out of the way.

## Design Philosophy — Techwear Direction

RYDR draws from the **Techwear** movement: the intersection of functional design, technical materials, and urban aesthetics. Inspiration comes from brands like CAKE and Ryvid, and principles aligned with Dieter Rams' functionalist approach.

### Core Techwear Principles for RYDR

**1. Technical Materials**
- Outerwear: 380gsm heavyweight cotton fleece — durable, preshrunk, colorfast
- Tees: 220gsm preshrunk ringspun cotton — structured, breathable, built for repeated wear
- Future drops: explore waterproof and wind-resistant shell fabrics

**2. Modular & Functional Design**
- Multi-pocket architecture with zip closures on all outerwear
- Adjustable systems: cinch cords, drawcords, structured hems
- No decorative elements — every design decision serves a function

**3. Palette Discipline**
- Black (`#0d0d0d`) is the base material — not a color choice
- Neon yellow (`#f5ff00`) is the signal — used exclusively for brand marks and functional accents
- Electric orange (`#ff3c00`) for "Limited" and "Bestseller" badges only
- No gradients, no noise, no deviation

**4. Structured Silhouettes**
- Oversized where mobility demands it (shoulders, body)
- Structured where presence demands it (collar, cuffs, hem)
- Pieces that look intentional at speed — not relaxed or casual

### Anti-references (Techwear edition)
- No Gore-Tex cosplay — RYDR is streetwear first, not outdoor gear
- No excessive branding — the mark is restrained, placement is precise
- No athleisure crossover — this is urban, not gym

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA target (4.5:1 body text minimum).
- Reduced motion support for all entrance animations.
- The rider community crosses multiple languages and regions (CDMX, NY, Brussels, Monterrey represented in testimonials) — copy should stay legible in translation, no idiom-dependent wordplay.
